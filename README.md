1. Dual-Token Architecture — Canonical Definition
Dual-token architecture is the structural separation of ecological truth from economic
activity through the use of two distinct, non-interchangeable instruments with irreducible
roles.
Within u-Kama:
• Bio Dynamic Regeneration (BDR) functions exclusively as a non-financial
ecological reserve asset that records witnessed ecological continuity over time.
• BioDollar (BIO) functions as the system’s utility and participation token, enabling
access, coordination, obligation, and economic activity.
No quantitative, ratio-based, redeemable, or collateral relationship may exist between BDR
and BIO.
BDR does not back, peg, collateralise, or authorise BIO issuance.
BIO absorbs all economic volatility and stress so that BDR remains indifferent to markets.
This separation is absolute and non-negotiable.
2. Ecological Difficulty Adjustment (EDA) — Canonical
Definition
Ecological Difficulty Adjustment (EDA) is a protocol-level issuance governor that
progressively increases the burden of qualification for new BDR issuance as ecological
integrity improves.
EDA is driven by biophysical reality, not by time, funding needs, market demand, or price
signals.
As ecosystems mature:
• Regenerative gains become smaller and harder to achieve.
• Reversal risk increases.
• Verification intensity and temporal windows lengthen.
• Qualification thresholds tighten asymptotically toward zero issuance.
EDA ensures that accumulated BDR becomes increasingly scarce because ecological success
has been achieved, not because of administrative caps or financial incentives.

4. Constraint Without Linkage Clause — Canonical
Clause
Constraint Without Linkage is the governing rule that ecological truth may constrain
economic participation without ever being converted into a financial reference.
Under this clause:
• BDR units, balances, or valuations may never determine BIO issuance quantities.
• No peg, exchange rate, reserve ratio, or redemption pathway may exist between BDR
and BIO.
• Ecological success tightens participation conditions rather than expanding economic
entitlement.
BDR remains indifferent to financial activity.
BIO absorbs all economic stress.
This clause is absolute and overrides optimisation, convenience, and growth considerations.
5. Proof of Protection (PoP) and Proof of Regeneration
(PoR) — State-Machine Doctrine
Proof of Protection (PoP) and Proof of Regeneration (PoR) operate as Boolean state
machines that recognise ecological truth as discrete transitions, not continuous scores.
• Proof of Protection recognises restraint.
It functions as a negative constraint on ecological continuity, determining whether a
witnessed interval of ecological time remains intact under defined stress conditions.

• Proof of Regeneration recognises net ecological state improvement that persists

It explicitly does not reward effort, spend, intent, labour, inputs, or compliance.
Within these state machines:
• Truth is binary at the protocol layer.
• Ambiguity increases constraint.
• Absence defaults to failure unless explicitly permitted.

6. Sacred Access Doctrine — Canonical Doctrine
Sacred Access Doctrine governs the treatment of sacred knowledge, places, and practices
within u-Kama.
Sacred elements may enter the system only by explicit permission of recognised custodians
or elders within the community.
Access remains:
• Rare
• Selective
• Trust-qualified
• Revocable
Financial contribution, participation, or tenure confers no entitlement.
Sacred knowledge may never be paywalled, gamified, branded, commodified, or traded.
Silence remains a protected and legitimate choice.
7. Trias Testium — Principle of Independent Witnesses
Trias Testium is the principle that ecological truth may be recognised only when preserved
by three independent, non-sovereign witnesses:
1. A witness that remembers (machines and records).
2. A witness that interprets (named human roles bound through time).
3. A witness that contests coherence (systems that preserve contradiction and
uncertainty).
